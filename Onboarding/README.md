## ‚è≥ Task Prioritization Guide

Managing your tasks well is just as important as completing them.  
Use this guide to decide what to work on first and how to stay on track.

### üîë Priority Levels
- **High (üö® Critical)** ‚Üí Deadline within 1‚Äì2 days, or a blocker for the team.  
- **Medium (‚ö° Important)** ‚Üí Contributes to weekly goals, but not urgent.  
- **Low (üå± Nice-to-have)** ‚Üí Long-term or optional improvements.  

### üóÇÔ∏è How to Prioritize
1. **Check deadlines first** ‚Üí Always deliver on time.  
2. **Identify dependencies** ‚Üí If your task blocks others, finish it first.  
3. **Follow the 80/20 rule** ‚Üí Focus on tasks that give the most impact.  
4. **Communicate early** ‚Üí If blocked, raise it in your **Onboarding Checklist Issue**.  

### ‚úÖ Daily Flow
- Start day by reviewing **High-priority tasks**.  
- Allocate ~70% of your time to high/medium, ~30% to learning & improvements.  
- Update your progress in the **Weekly Progress Form**.  

### üìå Example
- üö® **Submit Weekly Progress Form (Friday 5 PM)** ‚Üí High  
- ‚ö° **Research AR tutorial links (due in 3 days)** ‚Üí Medium  
- üå± **Polish README formatting** ‚Üí Low  

üí° *Tip: Always finish High before Medium, Medium before Low.*
